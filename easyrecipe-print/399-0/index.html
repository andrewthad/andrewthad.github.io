Braised Winter Chicken with Rice
Prep time:  15 mins
Cook time:  45 mins
Total time:  1 hour
Serves: Serves 4
 
Braised Winter Chicken and Rice is a hearty winter meal, made extra tasty with a secret ingredient - orange zest and juice!
Ingredients
  • • 6 boneless, skinless chicken thighs
  • • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • • 1 medium onion, diced
  • • 2 carrots, peeled and thinly sliced
  • • 1 celery stalk, thinly sliced
  • • ½ teaspoon dried thyme
  • • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • • juice of one orange
  • • zest of one orange
  • • 1 14-oz can diced tomatoes with juices
  • • rice or barley for serving (I used a blend of brown and black rice)
Instructions
  1. Heat a pot over medium heat and add the olive oil. When the oil is hot, add the onion, carrots, celery, dried thyme, and a pinch of salt. Cook until the vegetables start to soften, about 3 minutes.
  2. Add the chicken and cook until the bottom of the pan has turned a dark brown, about 4 minutes longer. Add the garlic, orange juice, and tomatoes with their juices and bring to a simmer, scraping up browned bits from the bottom of the pan. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer the stew until the chicken is tender, about 30 minutes.
  3. Use tongs to transfer the chicken thighs to a large plate and let cool slightly. Cut or shred the chicken into bite-sized pieces. Return the chicken to the pan and stir in the orange zest.
  4. Scoop cooked rice or barley into bowls and top with the braised chicken.
